Constructivism was an abstract architectural and artistic philosophy. While rejecting styalization and overly-decorate art the moveemnt favored the use of industrial materials. Constructivism originated in Russia around 1915 by Alexander Rodchenko and Vladimir Tatlin. The artists who worked in this style and theme of work happened to also be more inclined to favor the use of art for propaganda as well as various social purposes. As a result, Constructivism is commonly associated with the Russian avant-garde as well as the Bolsheviks.

In our modern world, we can see the influence that Constructivism has had on the art that ensued. Furthermore, Constructivist art and architecture greatly affected art movements of the 20th century. The Bauhaus and De Stijl movements were largely impacted by Constructivism. The effect did not stop there; in addition to arcitecture and art, Constructivism influenced dance, fashion, film, graphic design, industrial design, scultpture and theatre.

The main goal of Constructivism was to focus the composition on construction, hence the name being Constructivism. The goal of the work was not for visual appeal nor for the materials to be used in unusual ways. The limits and possibilities of the materials is was constrained and limited the work being made. Overall, Constructivism strived to replace the concern that artists had with composition with construction.
